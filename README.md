# Gottlieb-Chime-Resonator
This repository contains STL files for fabricating a chime resonator for a EM Gottlieb pinball machine.  The design is a close approximation based on photos with a ruler.  The Gottlieb chime unit consists of three notes; 5F#(739.99Hz), 5D#(622.25Hz), and 5C# (554.37Hz).  The frequency for each of the resonating cavities is much lower than the notes produced by the chimes, the frequencies calculated are 510, 456, and 411 Hz. I'm not an expert on acoustics, but I suspect the designer of this resonator wanted to enhance the lower harmonics to give the chimes a much richer tone. If you want a resonator at the base key frequencies, I have included the stl for those also, but they may not mount directly into a Gottlieb chime chassis, as they are much smaller in height and width.  
The files for the actual Gottlieb resonator are named chime_housing_upper_large_OS.stl  and chime_housing_lower__large.stl.  The smaller resonator files are chime_housing_upper.stl and chime_housing_lower_rev3.stl.  There is also an aperture riser is shared with each version that is glued to the top of the resonator, this file is named Aperture_riser.stl
The chimes are made from 1.5"(38.1mm) plate aluminum at .125"(3.175mm) thickness. This material can be found in most hardware stores.  Each chime is of different size and hole pattern.  All holes are 5/16"(8mm) diameter.
Key  Freq   length  Hole span on center 
5C#  554Hz  175mm    89mm (43mm from each end)
5D#  622Hz  161mm    86.5mm (37mm from each end)
5F#  740Hz  146mm    81mm (32.5mm from each end)
Each chime requires two rubber grommets which can be purchased from Marco Specialties (GTB-2752 - Chime grommet - Gottlieb) you might also look into ceiling fan blade grommets from your local hardware store.
